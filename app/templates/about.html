About

Federated Principal Component Analysis is a popular technique which can be used for dimensionality reduction and sample stratification of high dimensional (biological) data. Conceptually, PCA is the eigendecomposition of the covariance matrix. The eigenvectors and eigenvalues describe the principal axes of variation.

For more information on how to use this tool, please visit the dedicated Help page
